Quantifying Incarceration

According to statistics gathered by the Prison Policy Initiative in 2021, 968 per 100,000 people are under some form of incarceration in Georgia, the fourth highest rate of any state in the United States. Even more alarming about these statistics is that Black people make up 59% of Georgia's prison population and 51% of Georgia's jail population, even though they only make up 31% of Georgia's total population. Throughout history, people have relied on racism to justify the disproportionately high number of incarcerated Black Georgians, claiming that Black people are more prone to criminality. This project uses digital humanities methods to analyze data gathered from Georgia prison records from the one-hundred-year period from the end of the Civil War in 1865 to the beginning of President Lyndon B. Johnson's War on Crime in 1965 to demonstrate that the disproportionate number of incarcerated Black Georgians has nothing to do with inherent criminality and instead can be traced back to Georgia's complex history with racism and slavery.
